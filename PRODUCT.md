# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring teams evaluating Segun as a frontend engineer. They are scanning quickly for proof of craft, project quality, technical fluency, and whether it is worth opening the resume or starting a conversation.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to make Segun feel credible, memorable, and easy to act on. Success means a recruiter can quickly understand the role fit, inspect selected work, open the resume, and contact Segun without friction.

## Brand Personality

Precise, energetic, and technically fluent. The site should feel like a frontend engineer who cares about interaction quality, performance, and visual polish without losing clarity.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic developer-portfolio templates, slow flashy effects, SaaS landing-page cliches, cyberpunk styling, and playful visuals that make the work feel less serious. Do not let animation or spectacle hide the projects, resume, or contact path.

## Design Principles

- Lead with proof: recruiters should see project quality and frontend judgment quickly.
- Make motion earn attention: ambitious effects should clarify skill and craft, not decorate empty space.
- Keep the next action obvious: project preview, resume, and contact should stay easy to find.
- Feel fast and deliberate: every interaction should reinforce confidence.
- Preserve accessibility: reduced-motion users should still get a polished, complete experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect reduced motion, keep contrast strong on the dark interface, preserve keyboard access for navigation and previews, and avoid effects that require precise pointer movement to understand the page.
