# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and clients evaluating Segun as a frontend engineer. They arrive with limited time, scan for taste and implementation credibility, and need to decide whether the work feels trustworthy enough to open a conversation. Technical peers are secondary users who should still see evidence of craft, motion judgment, responsive care, and modern frontend fluency.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to make Segun feel hireable, memorable, and technically credible. Success means a visitor quickly understands the kind of work he builds, sees proof through real projects, and feels enough confidence to view a project or start contact.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, premium, precise. The tone should feel confident without being loud, expressive without becoming decorative, and technical without hiding behind developer clichés.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing-page tropes, beige editorial templates, neon crypto aesthetics, fake dashboard hero metrics, identical icon-card grids, decorative glassmorphism, and AI-looking portfolio layouts with vague claims. Do not make the page feel like a template gallery.

## Design Principles

Show proof before persuasion.
Make the craft visible, but keep the message easy to scan.
Use motion as direction and depth, not decoration.
Let the portfolio feel like a finished product, not a resume wrapper.
Keep contact and project exploration obvious at every viewport.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support keyboard navigation, clear focus states, readable contrast, responsive layouts from mobile to desktop, and reduced-motion preferences. Rich motion is welcome for the default experience, but reduced-motion users should still get a composed and complete page.
